Overview

David Keith Lynch (born January 20, 1946) is an American filmmaker, painter, musician, singer, sound designer, photographer, and actor. He is best known for writing and directing films such as Eraserhead (1977), The Elephant Man (1980), Blue Velvet (1986), and Mulholland Drive (2001), which are often regarded by critics as among the best films of their times, and for his television series Twin Peaks (1990–1991, 2017). These works led to him being labeled "the first popular Surrealist" by film critic Pauline Kael. A recipient of an Academy Honorary Award in 2019, he has received three Academy Award nominations for Best Director, and has won the César Award for Best Foreign Film twice, as well as the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival and a Golden Lion award for lifetime achievement at the Venice Film Festival. He has been described by The Guardian as "the most important director of this era", while AllMovie called him "the Renaissance man of modern American filmmaking".

    Milestones

  • 1977 - Debuted his first feature film "Eraserhead" which became an instant cult classic.
  • 1980 - Made an academy awards nominated film "The Elephant Man" which is about the life of John Merrick played by John Hurt.
  • 1984 - Adapted one of the best science-fiction novel "Dune" by Frank Herbert to the big screen
  • 1986 - Wrote and directed one of the best masterpiece of all time "Blue Velvet" and FRANK BOOTH
  • 1990 - Started one of the best TV series of all time with Mark Frost "Twin Peaks"
  • 1990 - Also made a feature film staring Nicolas Cage and Laura Dern "Wild at Heart"
  • 1992 - Made a prequel film to the successful TV series "Twin Peaks Fire Walk With Me"
  • 1997 - Wrote and directed one of the most underrated surreal film of all time "Lost Highway"
  • 1999 - Directed one of his less surreal - more normal film, and is still excellent "The Straight Story"
  • 2001 - Created a top-notch masterpiece that captures the dream logic in a surrealistic way "Mulholland Drive"
  • 2006 - Made a joyride of a film and one of the top performance of Laura Dern "Inland Empire"
  • 2017 - Revived his masterpiece TV series "Twin Peaks"
